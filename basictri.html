Factoring By basic trinomial

Basic trinomials have the form ax^2 + bx + c, and they can be factored by finding two binomials that multiply to the trinomial. Example 1: Factor the trinomial 4x^2 + 12x + 9. Step 1: Find two binomials that multiply to the trinomial. (2x + 3)(2x + 3) Result: (2x + 3)^2 Example 2: Factor the trinomial x^2 - 5x + 6. Step 1: Find two binomials that multiply to the trinomial. (x - 2)(x - 3) Result: (x - 2)(x - 3)

Completely Factoring Any Polynomial:

To completely factor any factorable polynomial, follow these steps: 1. Factor out the GCF, if possible. 2. Factor by grouping if the polynomial has four or more terms. 3. Factor basic trinomials if there are any. 4. Continue factoring until you can no longer simplify. Example 1: Completely factor the polynomial 2x^3 - 6x^2 + 8x - 24. Step 1: Factor out the GCF, which is 2. 2(x^3 - 3x^2 + 4x - 12) Step 2: Group and factor. 2(x^3 - 3x^2) + 2(4x - 12) 2x^2(x - 3) + 2(4x - 12) Step 3: Factor a basic trinomial. 2x^2(x - 3) + 2(4(x - 3)) Step 4: Factor out (x - 3) from both terms. 2(x - 3)(x^2 + 2) Example 2: Completely factor the polynomial 3x^4 - 12x^3 + 9x^2. Step 1: Factor out the GCF, which is 3x^2. 3x^2(x^2 - 4x + 3) Step 2: Factor a basic trinomial. 3x^2(x^2 - 4x + 3) 3x^2(x - 3)(x - 1) Now you have a completely factored expression: 3x^2(x - 3)(x - 1).
