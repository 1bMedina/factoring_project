Factoring by Grouping

Factoring by grouping involves grouping terms in the polynomial and finding common factors within these groups.

Example 1: Factor the polynomial 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 3x^2 - 6x. Step 1: Group the terms with common factors. (4x^3 - 2x^2) + (3x^2 - 6x) Step 2: Factor out the GCF from each group. 2x^2(2x - 1) + 3x(2x - 1) Step 3: Notice that both terms now have the factor (2x - 1) in common. Result: (2x^2 + 3x)(2x - 1)
