Introduction

Factoring polynomials is a fundamental skill that allows us to simplify and solve equations. Factoring is the process of expressing a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials. This guide will cover three important topics in factoring:

  • 1. Factoring by GCF (Greatest Common Factor)
  • 2. Factoring by Grouping
  • 3. Factoring Basic Trinomials

    • Additionally, we will explore how to completely factor any factorable polynomial and provide examples for each of the three methods. Definition of Factoring: Factoring is the process of breaking down a polynomial into a product of simpler polynomials or expressions. This simplification helps us solve equations, identify roots, and understand the behavior of polynomial functions.
