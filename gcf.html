Factoring by GCF

When factoring by GCF, we look for the largest common factor among all the terms in the polynomial and then divide each term by that factor. Example 1: Factor the polynomial 6x^3 - 12x^2 + 18x. Step 1: Find the GCF of all terms, which is 6x. Step 2: Divide each term by the GCF. (6x^3/6x) - (12x^2/6x) + (18x/6x) Result: x^2 - 2x + 3 Example 2: Factor the polynomial 15a^2b - 10ab^2 + 25ab. Step 1: Find the GCF of all terms, which is 5ab. Step 2: Divide each term by the GCF. (15a^2b/5ab) - (10ab^2/5ab) + (25ab/5ab) Result: 3a - 2b + 5
